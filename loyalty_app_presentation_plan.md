# Biggs Loyalty App Presentation Plan
### Marketing Team | Restaurant Mobile Application

## Presentation Structure

### Phase 1 — Opening

---

#### Slide 1: Title Slide

**Goal:** Set the tone — this is a forward-looking initiative.

**Content:**
- Restaurant name and logo
- Presentation title: *"Next Level: Our Loyalty App"*
- Marketing team name and date

> **Tip:** Use a bold visual — a clean phone mockup or brand color background to grab attention immediately.

### Phase 2 — Current State

---

#### Slide 3: Existing Loyalty Flow

**Goal:** Show what's already working — give credit to the existing system.

**Content:**
- Current loyalty card or program flow (reference existing flow diagram)
- Pain points of the current approach
- The gap between what customers want and what we currently offer

> **Tip:** Use a simple before/after or flow diagram. Reference the existing flow your team already has to maintain continuity.

---

### Phase 3 — The App

---

#### Slide 4: Introducing the Loyalty App

**Goal:** First reveal — create excitement for the solution.

**Content:**
- App overview and vision statement
- Target customer profile
- How the app connects to and enhances the existing loyalty program

> **Tip:** Use one clean hero image of the app. Let the visual speak first, then explain the details.

---

#### Slide 5: Feature — Store Locator

**Goal:** Show how the app helps customers find us.

**Content:**
- What it does: Interactive map showing all restaurant branches
- Customer benefit: Easy discovery, no need to Google — it's all in one place
- Business benefit: Drives foot traffic, especially for new or less-known branches

> **Tip:** Include a screen mockup of the map view. Keep it clean and recognizable.

---

#### Slide 6: Feature — Menu

**Goal:** Highlight the convenience of browsing the menu digitally.

**Content:**
- What it does: Full digital menu with categories, photos, and descriptions
- Customer benefit: Browse before visiting, plan orders ahead of time
- Business benefit: Sets expectations, reduces order time, increases upsell opportunity

> **Tip:** Show a sample menu screen. Highlight food photography — visuals sell.

---

#### Slide 7: Feature — Promotions

**Goal:** Demonstrate how the app becomes a marketing channel.

**Content:**
- What it does: Push notifications and in-app display of ongoing promotions
- Customer benefit: Never miss a deal, always in the loop
- Business benefit: Direct communication channel, increased promo awareness and redemption

> **Tip:** Show a promo banner or notification mockup. Emphasize the real-time nature of updates.

---

#### Slide 8: Feature — View Available Vouchers

**Goal:** Show the tangible rewards customers can access.

**Content:**
- What it does: Displays all earned and available vouchers in a digital wallet
- Customer benefit: No more lost paper vouchers, easy to redeem at checkout
- Business benefit: Higher redemption rates, reduced friction at point-of-sale

> **Tip:** Show a voucher card UI — make it look desirable and easy to use.

---

#### Slide 9: Feature — View Current Points

**Goal:** Reinforce the loyalty loop and motivate repeat visits.

**Content:**
- What it does: Real-time display of accumulated loyalty points and tier status
- Customer benefit: Transparency and motivation to keep earning
- Business benefit: Drives repeat visits, strengthens emotional connection to the brand

> **Tip:** Show a points dashboard. If you have tiers (Silver, Gold, etc.), display them here to create aspiration.

---

### Phase 4 — Impact

---

#### Slide 10: Expected Impact & Benefits

**Goal:** Connect the app back to business results the team cares about.

**Content:**
- Improved customer retention rate
- Increased repeat visit frequency
- Higher voucher redemption and promo uptake
- Stronger brand engagement and digital presence

> **Tip:** Use projected numbers or benchmarks from similar restaurants if available. Simple bar charts or icon-based stats work well here.

---

#### Slide 11: Rollout Plan & Next Steps

**Goal:** Give the team a clear, executable path forward.

**Content:**

| Phase | Activity | Timeline |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Phase 1: Build | App development, QA, internal testing | Month 1–3 |
| Phase 2: Launch | Soft launch at pilot branch, collect feedback | Month 4 |
| Phase 3: Scale | Full rollout, marketing push, loyalty migration | Month 5–6 |

**Metrics to Track:**
- App download rate
- Daily/Monthly active users
- Voucher redemption rate
- Points earned per customer
- Repeat visit frequency (app users vs. non-users)

> **Tip:** A simple 3-phase roadmap (Build → Launch → Scale) keeps this clear and actionable for all stakeholders.

---

### Phase 5 — Close

---

#### Slide 12: Q&A + Call to Action

**Goal:** End with energy and a clear ask.

**Content:**
- Summary vision: *"One app. Every reason to come back."*
- Open floor for questions
- State what you need: approval, budget sign-off, or team alignment

> **Tip:** End with a single bold statement — your "why this matters" closing line. Make the last thing they hear memorable.

---

## Key Messaging Points

Throughout the presentation, anchor your talking points around these three themes:

1. **Convenience** — The app puts everything the customer needs in one place.
2. **Loyalty** — Points, vouchers, and promotions keep customers coming back.
3. **Growth** — This is not just an app; it's a new revenue and retention channel for the restaurant.

---

## Design Notes for Slides

- Use phone mockups for all feature slides to make the app feel real and tangible
- Keep each slide to one main idea — avoid information overload
- Lean on brand colors throughout for a cohesive, professional look
- Use short headlines (5 words or fewer) and let visuals carry the weight
- Maintain consistent font sizes: large headers, readable body text
